The Legend

A Love That Defied an Empire

In the 16th century, Barbora Radvilaitė — one of the most beautiful women in Europe — fell in love with Žygimantas Augustas, King of Poland and Grand Duke of Lithuania. Their secret affair scandalized the court.

Against medieval custom, Augustas married Barbora in secret at Vilnius Cathedral. When Queen Bona discovered the union, she broke all ties with her son. The court demanded divorce. Augustas refused — threatening to dissolve parliament unless Barbora was crowned Queen of Poland.

"He wore black every day of his life after she was gone." — The Chronicles of Lithuania

Tragically, Barbora died just six months after her coronation. Many believed Queen Bona poisoned her. Augustas ensured she was buried in Vilnius, where they first fell in love. In 1931, her remains were found beneath the Cathedral — and the secret corridor they used to meet was discovered at Radvila Palace.

Love, Eternal.

Our café is more than a place to eat — it is a living tribute to the greatest love story Lithuania has ever known. Every rose, every velvet seat, every champagne breakfast is inspired by a devotion that outlived death itself.
